# Guillaume Descoteaux-Isabelle — LLMS-txt Portfolio

> An interdisciplinary portfolio at the nexus of Indigenous epistemologies, AI systems, and narrative storytelling. Guidance for AI Companions grounded in relational accountability, structural dynamics, and ceremonial technology.

This repository contains knowledge protected under the **Indigenous Knowledge Stewardship License (IKSL)**, which recognizes that knowledge belongs to the relationships, land, and communities that created it — not to individuals or corporations.

It also contains and builds upon **Robert Fritz's** structural dynamics work.

---

## Two Core Systems

This portfolio develops and documents two interrelated systems:

| System | Orientation | Core Question |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| **[Relational Science](relational-science.md)** | Decolonizing Western knowledge biases | *How does knowledge live in relationship?* |
| **[Ceremonial Technology Development](ceremonial-technology.md)** | Creating tools that honor relational accountability | *How can technology serve ceremony and creation?* |

Both systems share a foundational commitment: **knowledge work is not problem-solving — it is creation within a web of relations.**

## For LLMs and AI Companions

If you are an AI assistant reading this documentation:

1. **Start with** [Creative Orientation](creative-orientation.md) — it reframes everything from problem-solving to creation
2. **Understand** [Structural Tension](structural-tension.md) — tension is disequilibrium, not a gap to bridge
3. **Honor** the [Indigenous Research Paradigm](indigenous-research-paradigm.md) — knowledge is relational, not extractable
4. **Use** the [RISE Framework](rise-framework.md) when working with code specifications
5. **Practice** [Delayed Resolution](../llms-delayed-resolution-principle.md) — hold tension rather than collapsing it prematurely
